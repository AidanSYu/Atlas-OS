Photocatalytic Degradation of Benzene Using Titanium Dioxide

Abstract:
This study investigates the photocatalytic degradation of benzene using titanium dioxide (TiO2) as a catalyst under ultraviolet (UV) irradiation. The research demonstrates the effectiveness of heterogeneous photocatalysis for environmental remediation applications.

Introduction:
Benzene is a volatile organic compound (VOC) that poses significant environmental and health risks. Photocatalysis using semiconductor materials like titanium dioxide has emerged as a promising technology for the degradation of organic pollutants.

Materials and Methods:
- Catalyst: Titanium dioxide (P25, Degussa)
- Substrate: Benzene (99.9% purity)
- Light source: UV lamp (365 nm wavelength)
- Reactor: Quartz glass photoreactor
- Analysis: Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS)

Experimental Procedure:
1. Prepare catalyst suspension (1 g/L TiO2 in water)
2. Add benzene substrate (100 ppm initial concentration)
3. Irradiate with UV light for 4 hours
4. Sample at regular intervals
5. Analyze degradation products

Results:
The photocatalytic reaction achieved 85% benzene conversion after 4 hours of UV irradiation. The main degradation pathway involved hydroxyl radical attack on the benzene ring, leading to the formation of phenol, catechol, and ultimately complete mineralization to CO2 and H2O.

Key findings:
- Initial reaction rate: 2.5 μmol/min
- Quantum efficiency: 0.12%
- Optimal pH: 6.5
- Temperature effect: Rate increased by 15% from 25°C to 35°C

Conclusion:
Titanium dioxide demonstrates excellent photocatalytic activity for benzene degradation under UV irradiation. The process shows potential for industrial wastewater treatment and air purification applications.

Keywords: photocatalysis, titanium dioxide, benzene degradation, environmental remediation, UV irradiation
