SAC305 Reflow Soldering: Process Window and Defect Modes

SAC305 is the dominant lead-free solder alloy for surface-mount PCB assembly.
Its composition is 96.5% tin, 3.0% silver, and 0.5% copper, with a liquidus
temperature near 217 C and a typical reflow peak temperature of 235-245 C.

The standard SAC305 reflow profile has four zones: preheat (room temp to 150 C),
soak (150-180 C for 60-120 seconds), reflow (above 217 C for 30-90 seconds of
time-above-liquidus, with peak near 240 C), and cooling (ramp-down below 5 C/s
to prevent thermal shock).

Critical Process Parameters

- Peak temperature: 230-250 C; below this, solder joints do not fully wet
  the pads; above it, component damage and warping occur
- Time-above-liquidus (TAL): 30-90 seconds; shorter than 30s risks cold
  joints, longer than 90s accelerates intermetallic compound growth and
  causes excessive voids
- Ramp rate: 1-3 C/s on heating, ≤5 C/s on cooling; faster rates cause
  thermal-shock tombstoning on small chip components
- Soak time: 60-120s; this equalizes temperature across the board and
  activates flux; skipping it causes uneven wetting across mixed component
  sizes

Common Defect Modes

- Cold joint: insufficient peak temperature or TAL; visible as dull, grainy
  solder surface with poor wetting fillet
- Overshoot damage: peak temperature above 250 C causes component body
  damage, PCB delamination, and BGA ball collapse
- Tombstoning: asymmetric heating on 0201/0402 chip components causes one
  end to solder before the other; surface tension lifts the component off
  the pad
- Head-in-pillow: reflow profile doesn't provide enough energy to coalesce
  the BGA ball with the pad solder; joint looks correct visually but has
  no electrical contact
- Excessive voids: long TAL or high peak temperature traps outgassing from
  the flux inside the solder joint; X-ray inspection shows bubbles

Physics-Based Monitoring

The thermal profile at each reflow pass is a 1D time-series of board
temperature. The manufacturing world model treats this time-series as the
observation space and forecasts the next reflow pass from recent history.
Change-point detection flags profile drift (oven aging, conveyor speed
changes) before it produces a batch of defective boards. Anomaly detection
flags individual passes that deviate from the fleet baseline.
