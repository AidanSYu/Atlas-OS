Reference Compendium: Common Small Molecules for Property Prediction Validation

This document provides verified molecular properties for commonly queried molecules, serving as ground truth for computational property prediction validation.

==========================================================================
1. CAFFEINE
==========================================================================
Name: 1,3,7-Trimethylxanthine
SMILES: CN1C=NC2=C1C(=O)N(C(=O)N2C)C
Molecular Formula: C8H10N4O2
CAS Number: 58-08-2

Properties:
  Molecular Weight: 194.19 g/mol
  LogP: -0.07 (hydrophilic, unusual for a drug)
  TPSA: 58.44 Angstrom^2
  HBD: 0 (no N-H or O-H groups)
  HBA: 6
  Rotatable Bonds: 0 (rigid planar structure)
  QED: ~0.53

Lipinski Assessment: PASS (0 violations)
Toxicity Alerts: None (clean)
PAINS: None

Pharmacology: Adenosine A1 and A2A receptor antagonist. Rapidly absorbed orally (Tmax ~45 min), 99% bioavailability. Metabolized by CYP1A2 to paraxanthine (84%), theobromine (12%), and theophylline (4%).

Note: Caffeine's LogP is near zero (-0.07), making it one of the most hydrophilic orally bioavailable drugs. This is due to extensive N-methylation and the absence of ionizable groups at physiological pH. Its excellent oral absorption despite low LogP is attributed to its small size and membrane permeability via passive transcellular diffusion.

2. PENICILLIN G (Benzylpenicillin)
==========================================================================
Name: Benzylpenicillin
SMILES: CC1(C)SC2C(NC(=O)CC3=CC=CC=C3)C(=O)N2C1C(=O)O
Molecular Formula: C16H18N2O4S
CAS Number: 61-33-6

Properties:
  Molecular Weight: 334.39 g/mol
  LogP: 1.83
  TPSA: 112.01 Angstrom^2
  HBD: 2
  HBA: 6
  Rotatable Bonds: 4
  QED: ~0.65

Lipinski Assessment: PASS (0 violations)
Toxicity Alerts: Beta-lactam ring could be considered a mild structural alert due to its electrophilic carbonyl, but is the intended pharmacophore. Standard SMARTS-based toxicity screens should NOT flag it unless specifically looking for strained lactams.
PAINS: None

Pharmacology: Inhibits bacterial transpeptidases (PBPs) by acylating the active-site serine. Poor oral bioavailability (15-30%) due to acid hydrolysis of the beta-lactam ring in gastric conditions. Primarily administered parenterally.

3. METFORMIN
==========================================================================
Name: N,N-Dimethylbiguanide
SMILES: CN(C)C(=N)NC(=N)N
Molecular Formula: C4H11N5
CAS Number: 657-24-9

Properties:
  Molecular Weight: 129.16 g/mol
  LogP: -1.43 (very hydrophilic)
  TPSA: 91.49 Angstrom^2
  HBD: 3
  HBA: 5
  Rotatable Bonds: 2
  QED: ~0.30 (low drug-likeness score despite being #1 prescribed diabetes drug worldwide)

Lipinski Assessment: PASS (0 violations)
Toxicity Alerts: None
PAINS: None

Key Insight: Metformin's very low QED (0.30) illustrates a critical limitation of property-based drug-likeness scores. QED penalizes metformin for its very low MW, very low LogP, and high polar surface area fraction. Yet metformin has been prescribed to >150 million patients globally and has an excellent safety profile spanning 60+ years of clinical use.

This demonstrates that QED and Lipinski assessments are useful FILTERS but should never be used as absolute GO/NO-GO criteria. The Discovery OS should present these as data points for human interpretation, not as verdicts.

4. PACLITAXEL (Taxol)
==========================================================================
Name: Paclitaxel
SMILES: CC1=C2C(C(=O)C3(C(CC4C(C3C(C(C2(C)C)(CC1OC(=O)C(C5=CC=CC=C5)NC(=O)C6=CC=CC=C6)O)OC(=O)C7=CC=CC=C7)(CO4)OC(=O)C)O)C)OC(=O)C
Molecular Formula: C47H51NO14
CAS Number: 33069-62-4

Properties:
  Molecular Weight: 853.91 g/mol
  LogP: 3.96
  TPSA: 221.29 Angstrom^2
  HBD: 4
  HBA: 15
  Rotatable Bonds: 14
  QED: ~0.06 (very low)

Lipinski Assessment: FAIL (3 violations: MW>500, TPSA>140, HBA>10)
Toxicity Alerts: Contains ester groups susceptible to hydrolysis but no classic SMARTS toxicophores
PAINS: None

Key Insight: Paclitaxel is one of the most important chemotherapy drugs in history (breast cancer, ovarian cancer, NSCLC, Kaposi's sarcoma) yet it catastrophically fails Lipinski's Rule of Five and has a QED of 0.06. It is administered intravenously, completely bypassing the oral absorption barrier that Lipinski's rules were designed to predict.

This is the canonical example of why Lipinski violations should be flagged but NOT used to exclude compounds from consideration when the route of administration is parenteral.

5. ETHANOL
==========================================================================
Name: Ethanol
SMILES: CCO
Molecular Formula: C2H6O
CAS Number: 64-17-5

Properties:
  Molecular Weight: 46.07 g/mol
  LogP: -0.31
  TPSA: 20.23 Angstrom^2
  HBD: 1
  HBA: 1
  Rotatable Bonds: 0
  QED: ~0.41

Lipinski Assessment: PASS (0 violations)
Toxicity Alerts: None by SMARTS screening (although ethanol is obviously toxic at high doses)
PAINS: None

Key Insight: Ethanol passes all computational drug-likeness filters cleanly, yet is one of the most toxic substances by volume consumed globally (responsible for ~3 million deaths annually per WHO). This illustrates that structural alert screening captures REACTIVE toxicophores (electrophiles, mutagens) but does NOT capture PHARMACOLOGICAL toxicity (CNS depression, organ damage from chronic exposure). The check_toxicity tool screens for structural reactivity, not systemic toxicology.
