Project: Xyloside GAG Primer Discovery Campaign
Owner: Atlas Discovery Team
Date: 2026-04-01

Primary objective:
Discover novel beta-D-xylopyranoside derivatives that efficiently prime
glycosaminoglycan (GAG) biosynthesis for use as chemical biology probes
and potential therapeutic leads in fibrosis and inflammation.

Scientific rationale:
Xylosides act as artificial acceptors for galactosyltransferase II (GalT-II),
hijacking the GAG initiation machinery from endogenous proteoglycan core proteins.
The resulting free GAG chains (primarily chondroitin sulfate and heparan sulfate)
can modulate cell signaling, ECM remodeling, and inflammatory cascades. Productive
primers require an aglycone moiety that balances membrane uptake with sufficient
polarity for intracellular trafficking to the Golgi.

Target profile:
- Molecular class: beta-D-xylopyranoside O-linked glycosides
- Mechanism: competitive substrate for GalT-II in the Golgi
- Desired chain type: chondroitin sulfate (CS) and/or heparan sulfate (HS)
- Application: chemical biology tool compounds; potential anti-fibrotic leads

Property constraints:
- Molecular weight: 180 - 450 Da
- LogP: 0.5 - 3.0 (balance uptake vs. aqueous solubility)
- TPSA: 40 - 160 (Golgi trafficking requires moderate polarity)
- Hydrogen bond donors: <= 6
- Hydrogen bond acceptors: <= 12
- Rotatable bonds: <= 8

Safety constraints:
- No PAINS motifs (pan-assay interference)
- No reactive toxicophores (Michael acceptors, epoxides, azides)
- Avoid nitro aromatics (mutagenicity concern)
- Prefer low hERG risk for any therapeutic follow-up
- Prefer low DILI risk

Success criteria:
- GAG priming EC50 < 50 uM in CHO cell assay
- CS/HS ratio skew measurable by disaccharide HPLC
- QED >= 0.4 (drug-likeness baseline)
- SA score <= 6 (synthetically tractable)
- Clean structural alert profile (zero PAINS, zero toxicophore flags)
