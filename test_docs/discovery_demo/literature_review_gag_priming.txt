Literature Review: Xyloside-Mediated GAG Priming

Finding 1: Aglycone hydrophobicity determines priming efficiency
- Okayama et al. demonstrated that aromatic aglycones prime GAG chains
  10-100x more efficiently than aliphatic ones. The aromatic ring provides
  critical pi-stacking interactions with GalT-II active site residues.
- Optimal LogP range for cell-active xylosides: 0.8 - 2.5.
- Above LogP 3.0, compounds aggregate in aqueous media and give false
  positives in ELISA-based GAG quantification assays.

Finding 2: Aglycone structure controls CS vs HS chain selectivity
- Coumarin and methylcoumarin aglycones selectively prime chondroitin
  sulfate (CS) chains with >80% selectivity over heparan sulfate (HS).
- Simple phenyl and naphthyl aglycones produce mixed CS/HS populations.
- For anti-fibrotic applications, CS-selective primers are preferred because
  excess HS can activate FGF signaling and promote fibroblast proliferation.

Finding 3: Polar substituents improve pharmacokinetic properties
- Hydroxymethyl and carboxyl groups on the aglycone ring improve aqueous
  solubility 3-5 fold without significantly reducing priming potency.
- Methoxy groups provide moderate lipophilicity tuning and are metabolically
  stable in human hepatocyte assays (t1/2 > 4 hours).
- 3,4-dimethoxy pattern on phenyl aglycone shows enhanced Golgi retention.

Finding 4: Benzylic linkage improves metabolic stability
- O-benzyl xylosides show 2-3x longer half-life in microsomal stability
  assays compared to direct O-aryl xylosides.
- The methylene spacer reduces susceptibility to glucuronidation.
- Trade-off: slightly reduced priming potency (1.5x shift in EC50).

Finding 5: Heteroaromatic aglycones as next-generation scaffolds
- Benzofuran-5-yl xyloside showed promising priming (EC50 = 28 uM) with
  reduced hERG liability compared to naphthyl (2-fold selectivity margin).
- Indanyl xylosides combine the hydrophobic contact surface of naphthyl
  with reduced planarity, potentially mitigating hERG risk.
- These scaffolds are underexplored and represent a key opportunity.

Finding 6: Drug-likeness metrics correlate with in vivo translation
- Xylosides with QED > 0.5 and SA score < 5 were 3x more likely to
  advance from in vitro hit to in vivo proof-of-concept.
- Lipinski rule-of-five compliance is a strong positive predictor for
  oral dosing feasibility in rodent fibrosis models.
