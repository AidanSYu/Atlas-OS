ADMET and Safety Triage Guide for Xyloside Campaign

Hard exclusions (automatic reject):
- Any PAINS structural alert (pan-assay interference)
- Nitro aromatic motifs (confirmed Ames-positive in prior campaign)
- Reactive warheads: Michael acceptors, epoxides, azides, acyl halides
- Predicted hERG risk = HIGH
- More than 2 toxicophore alerts from Brenk filter set

Soft penalties (deprioritize but do not reject):
- CYP3A4 inhibition: medium or high (metabolic drug interaction risk)
- Caco2 permeability: low (may indicate poor oral absorption)
- DILI risk: medium (liver toxicity concern for chronic dosing)
- SA score > 6 (difficult synthesis, but acceptable if compound is exceptional)

Preferred ADMET window:
- hERG risk: LOW
- DILI risk: LOW
- Caco2 permeability: MEDIUM or HIGH
- CYP3A4 inhibition: LOW
- Overall ADMET risk classification: LOW or MEDIUM

Known problematic motifs from XYL-Alpha-01:
- Flat polyaromatic systems (>= 3 fused rings): hERG liability
- Unsubstituted anilines: oxidative metabolism, potential genotoxicity
- Long alkyl chains (>= 6 carbons): aggregation, poor solubility
- Aldehyde groups: protein reactivity, instability

Structural alert priority for ranking:
- Zero alerts = top tier
- 1 soft alert = acceptable if potency and properties are strong
- 2+ alerts = deprioritize
- Any hard exclusion = reject regardless of other properties
