Assay Protocols for Xyloside GAG Primer Screening

Assay 1: GAG Priming ELISA (primary screen)
- Cell line: CHO-K1 (Chinese hamster ovary)
- Treatment: 48 hours, 6-point dose response (1 - 100 uM)
- Readout: total secreted GAG quantified by Blyscan sulfated GAG assay
- Positive control: 4-methylumbelliferyl-beta-D-xyloside (4-MU-Xyl) at 50 uM
- Pass threshold: EC50 < 50 uM with >= 2-fold GAG induction over DMSO
- Reject: cytotoxicity > 20% at screening concentration (MTT counter-screen)

Assay 2: CS/HS Selectivity Panel (secondary screen)
- Method: enzymatic digestion + disaccharide HPLC
- Enzymes: chondroitinase ABC (CS) and heparinases I/II/III (HS)
- Readout: CS disaccharide / HS disaccharide ratio
- Desired: CS/HS ratio > 3.0 for CS-selective primers
- Timeline: run on top 10 compounds from primary screen

Assay 3: Cytotoxicity Counter-Screen
- Method: MTT viability assay, 48 hours, matching dose range
- Cell line: CHO-K1 (same as primary) + HepG2 (hepatotoxicity)
- Reject threshold: > 30% viability loss at 100 uM in either line
- Flag: compounds with steep dose-response curves (Hill slope > 3)

Assay 4: Microsomal Stability (hit validation)
- Species: human and mouse liver microsomes
- Concentration: 1 uM, 60-minute incubation
- Readout: percent remaining at 60 min
- Target: > 50% remaining (human), > 30% remaining (mouse)
- Purpose: prioritize metabolically stable leads for in vivo studies

Decision tree:
1. Primary screen pass (EC50 < 50 uM, no cytotoxicity) -> proceed
2. CS selectivity panel -> rank by CS/HS ratio
3. Microsomal stability -> filter for in vivo candidates
4. Final ranking: composite of potency, selectivity, stability, and safety
