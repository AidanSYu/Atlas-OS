Prior Campaign Summary: XYL-Alpha-01

Campaign dates: 2025-09 through 2025-12
Compounds tested: 84 xyloside derivatives
Scaffold series: naphthyl, simple phenyl, and coumarin aglycones

Key results:
- 2-naphthyl xyloside was the most potent GAG primer (EC50 = 12 uM)
  but showed poor aqueous solubility (< 0.02 mg/mL) and hERG flag.
- 4-methylphenyl xyloside showed moderate priming (EC50 = 38 uM)
  with clean safety profile and good solubility.
- 4-nitrophenyl xyloside was active (EC50 = 22 uM) but triggered
  Ames-positive mutagenicity alert due to the nitro aromatic group.
  THIS MOTIF IS NOW EXCLUDED from all future campaigns.
- Coumarin-based xylosides selectively primed chondroitin sulfate chains
  over heparan sulfate, which is a desirable property for fibrosis models.

Observed liabilities:
- Naphthyl cores: high priming potency but poor solubility and hERG risk.
  The flat polyaromatic surface drives hERG channel binding.
- Unsubstituted phenyl: weakest priming (EC50 > 100 uM). Need substituents
  to improve aglycone-enzyme recognition.
- Nitro aromatics: mutagenicity concern confirmed in Ames panel. Hard exclusion.
- Highly lipophilic analogs (LogP > 3.5): erratic cell uptake, aggregation
  artifacts in the GAG ELISA assay.

Scaffold guidance for next round:
- Retain aromatic aglycones (required for GalT-II recognition).
- Favor mono- or di-substituted phenyl with polar groups (methoxy, hydroxymethyl,
  carboxyl) to improve solubility without sacrificing uptake.
- Explore heteroaromatic aglycones (benzofuran, indane) as naphthyl replacements
  with reduced planarity.
- Coumarin series warrants expansion for CS-selective priming.
- Benzylic linkage variants may improve metabolic stability vs direct O-aryl.

Do not repeat:
- Nitro-substituted aglycones (any position)
- Unsubstituted naphthyl without polar exit vectors
- Polyhalogenated aglycones (poor clearance in prior microsomal assay)
- Aglycones with MW contribution > 250 Da (total MW exceeded 450 cutoff)
