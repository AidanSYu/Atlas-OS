Design Hypotheses for XYL-Beta-01 Campaign

H1: Polar-substituted phenyl aglycones as balanced lead series
- Rationale: Prior campaign showed unsubstituted phenyl is too weak and
  naphthyl is too toxic. Methoxy, hydroxymethyl, and carboxyl substituents
  on phenyl should provide the hydrophobic contact needed for GalT-II
  recognition while improving solubility and reducing hERG risk.
- Key compounds to generate: 4-methoxyphenyl, 4-hydroxymethylphenyl,
  3,4-dimethoxyphenyl, and 4-carboxyphenyl xylosides.
- Expected outcome: EC50 20-50 uM with clean ADMET.

H2: Heteroaromatic aglycones as naphthyl replacements
- Rationale: Naphthyl gives the best potency but unacceptable hERG risk.
  Benzofuran and indane scaffolds provide similar hydrophobic surface area
  with reduced planarity, which should mitigate hERG channel binding.
- Key compounds: benzofuran-5-yl, indanyl xylosides.
- Expected outcome: Potency within 2x of naphthyl, hERG risk LOW.

H3: Coumarin series expansion for CS-selective priming
- Rationale: Literature confirms coumarin xylosides selectively prime CS
  chains. For fibrosis applications, CS selectivity is the key differentiator.
  Expanding methylcoumarin variants should map the SAR.
- Key compounds: 7-methylcoumarin-4-yl xyloside and analogs.
- Expected outcome: CS selectivity >80%, moderate potency.

H4: Benzylic linkage variants for metabolic stability
- Rationale: O-benzyl linkage showed 2-3x improved microsomal stability.
  If potency drop is modest, these may be superior for in vivo studies.
- Key compounds: benzyl, 4-methylbenzyl, 4-methoxybenzyl xylosides.
- Expected outcome: Improved PK profile, acceptable potency trade-off.

H5: Composite ranking over single-metric optimization
- Rationale: Prior campaign over-optimized for potency, leading to toxic
  naphthyl-heavy series. New ranking should weight safety (40%), drug-likeness
  (30%), and synthesis feasibility (30%) equally with potency.
- Implementation: Use QED x ADMET_clean x SA_feasible composite score.
