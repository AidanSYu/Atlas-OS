Synthesis and Supply Constraints for XYL-Beta-01

Route complexity limits:
- Maximum 4 linear steps from commercial building blocks.
- Xyloside glycosylation step counts as step 1 (Koenigs-Knorr or
  BF3-promoted glycosylation from peracetylated xylose).
- Aglycone preparation should be <= 3 steps.
- Avoid routes requiring cryogenic conditions or air-sensitive reagents.

Preferred building blocks:
- Peracetylated D-xylose: commercially available, $45/gram.
- Substituted phenols: broad catalog availability.
- Benzofuran-5-ol and 5-indanol: available from Sigma and Enamine.
- Coumarins: 4-methylumbelliferone widely available.

Scale assumptions:
- Initial screening: 5 mg per compound (sufficient for GAG ELISA).
- Hit confirmation: 25 mg for dose-response and selectivity panel.
- Lead optimization: 100 mg for microsomal stability and PK.

Budget:
- Per-compound cost target: under $200 for initial batch.
- Catalog compounds with make-on-demand: <= 3 week lead time preferred.

SA score guidance:
- SA score <= 4: prioritize (trivial synthesis).
- SA score 4-6: acceptable (standard medicinal chemistry).
- SA score > 6: deprioritize unless compound has exceptional properties.
- SA score > 8: reject (impractical for campaign timeline).
